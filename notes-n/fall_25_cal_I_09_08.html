2. The Velocity ProblemIf you watch the speedometer of a car as you drive in city traffic, you see that the speed doesn't stay the same for very long, because the velocity of the car is not constant. We assume from watching the speedometer that the car has a definite velocity at each moment, but how is the "instantaneous " velocity defined? If the distance fallen after t seconds is defined by s(t), then we can write s(t)=4.9t2 according to Galileo's observation.
